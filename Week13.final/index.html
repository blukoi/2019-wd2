American Cinematheque

Preserving Cinema

In the early days of film nothing was certain and film itself was fragile and easily damaged. Whether to age or the elements it wasn't unusual for a film to be lost. The Library of Congress has estimated that 75% of all silent films may be lost, including many works by the well-renowned likes of Georges Méliès and Alice Guy. That's a lot of history.

American Cinematheque is a group of filmmakers, writers, actors, and other industry members who want nothing more than to see the beauty of our film industry and history preserved so that it can be enjoyed by future generations.
