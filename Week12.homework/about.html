About Us

At American Cinematheque we have the honor and the privilege of protecting and restoring some of our most important cinematic treasures. Whether it's a film, a theater, or even a backlot, our goal is to save our film history so that the public can enjoy it for years to come.

We're proud to count among our devoted members the likes of Sydney Pollack, Martin Scorsese, Mike Nichols, Francis Coppola, William Friedkin, Melvin Van Peebles, Brian Grazer, Joe Dante, Paula Wagner, Steve Tisch, and many more.


Grauman's Egyptian Theater


Aero Theater

Built by Sid Grauman, The Egyptian Theater first opened for business in 1922. It was one of the first of a series of lavish movie palaces and is the site of the first-ever Hollywood premiere: Robin Hood in October 18, 1922. The world-famous Chinese Theater, also built by Grauman, would follow in 1927.

After the Egyptian fell into disrepair in the 80s and 90s we, American Cinematheque, purchased the theater from the city of Los Angeles for $1 under condition that the building be restored and reopened to the public. After a $12.8 million restoration the Egyptian was reopened in 1998. Projection, sound, seating, and circulation have all been updated to modern standards and in 2000 the theater was awarded the National Preservation Award from the National Trust for Historic Preservation.

Grauman's Egyptian Theater is currently open to the public.

Designed by P.M. Woolpert in 1939, the Aero opened in 1940 as a 24-hour venue to host the employees of Donald Wills Douglas and their families and friends. The theater featured modest accomodations but regularly scheduled double features.

After falling on hard times in the 90s the Aero was initially sought after by Robert Redford as another venue for his Sundance Group and their affiliate theaters. A deal was never struck and in 2005 we were able to purchase the Aero.

The Aero Theater in Santa Monica is currently open to the public.
